Alan Turing

Father of the modern computer

Summary

  • Born: June 23, 1912, Maida Vale, London, United Kingdom
  • Died: June 7, 1954, Wilmslow, United Kingdom
  • Education: Sherborne School
  • Siblings: John Turing
  • Parents: Julius Mathison Turing, Ethel Sara Stoney
  • Achievments:
    • Breaking the Enigma code
    • Contribution to the Chruch-Turing thesis
    • paper on Theory of Computation
    • Creation of on of the first modern computers
    • paper on Biomathematics

Timeline of Alan Turing

  • 1912 - Born in Maida Vale, London
  • 1931 - Studies at King's College, Cambridge for 3 years
  • 1936 - Publishes his paper "On Computable Numbers, with an Application to the Entscheidungsproblem" in which he introduces Turing Machines which later contributed to the Church-Turing thesis
  • 1936 - Starts to study under Alonzo Church at Princton University for 2 years
  • 1938 - Obtains his PhD from Department of Mathematics at Princton
  • 1940 - Installed the first bombe (An electromechanical machine used to break Enigma) with the help of his colleges
  • 1938 - Starts to work part-time with the Government Code and Cypher School to work on deciphering the Enigma machine
  • 1941 - Moves to Hut 8 to investigate on the German naval Enigma
  • 1941 - Proposes marriage to Hut 8 colleague Joan Clarke, which later discontinued due to Turing being Homosexual
  • 1942 - Moves to the US to work with the US naval army
  • 1942 - Devises a technique called Turingery for use against the Lorenz Cipher
  • 1942 - Helps design Delilah, a machine with the purpose of electronically ciphering speech
  • 1945 - Works on the design of ACE, the first detailed design of a stored-program computer
  • 1949 - Designs one of the earliest stored-program computers called Manchester Mark 1
  • 1950 - Proposes the idea of Turing test, an important contribution to the debate regarding artificial intelligence
  • 1952 - Publishes "The Chemical Basis of morphogenesis," a great discovery in mathematical biology
  • 1952 - Undergoes hormonal physical changes and chemical injections to "cure" his homosexuality
  • 1954 - Dies at the age of 41
